Codebook for UCI Dataset

The columns "Subjects" and "Activities" record what subject the measurements correspond to, and what activity the subject was performing at that time.
The rest of the variable names are recorded in the following format:
1) They start with either a) time or b) frequency, depending on the measurement. 2) Whether the measurements correspond to the a) Body or b) gravity is indicated. 3) Whether the measurements are from a) the accelerometer or b) Gryosope is indicated. 4) Whether a) Jerk or b) Magnitude is being measured. 5) Next, whether the mean, standard deviation (std), or mean frequency (denoted by meanfreq), which is the "Weighted average of the frequency components". 6) Finally, when applicable, whether the measurements correspond to the a) X, b) Y, or c) Z dimension. For more information.

For more information on the dataset variables, see the features_info.txt file avaiable at http://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/machine-learning-databases/00240/UCI%20HAR%20Dataset.zip

There are 81 total variables available (from the original dataset I selected all variables with mean or std in the variable name).
